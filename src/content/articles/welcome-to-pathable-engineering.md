Welcome to **Engineering at PathAble** — the public home for the technical work
behind PathAble's human-centered, AI-enhanced software. This is our first
article, and it doubles as a short statement of intent.

> This is a synthetic, illustrative pilot article. It contains no customer data,
> no protected health information, and no sensitive implementation details.

## Why a separate publication

PathAble's company website is the right place to learn about our product,
services, and pilot program. But it isn't the right place for architecture
notes, build logs, or open-source documentation. Engineering writing has a
different audience and a different cadence.

So we're carving out a distinct, clearly labeled space: an **Engineering** (or
_Labs_) property that links back to the company site, without competing with it.

## What you'll find here

We organize everything into four sections:

- **Field Notes** — short, practical observations from real work.
- **Architecture Notes** — system design, trade-offs, and decision records.
- **Practice Guides** — repeatable, opinionated how-tos.
- **Open Source** — the tools and docs we maintain in the open.

Each article carries a **maturity label** — _experimental_, _evolving_, or
_stable_ — so you always know how settled an idea is.

## The principles we hold ourselves to

1. **Accessible by default.** Every article is reviewed for semantics,
   color contrast, and alt text before it ships. Accessibility isn't a
   nice-to-have for us — it's the product.
2. **Synthetic examples only.** We never publish customer details or sensitive
   architecture. Examples are always illustrative.
3. **Honest about maturity.** We'd rather tell you something is experimental
   than over-promise.
4. **No unsupported claims.** We avoid overstating security, accessibility, or
   compliance guarantees.

## What's next

We'll start small and publish steadily. If you want to follow along, the
[RSS feed](/rss.xml) is the most reliable way — every article has a stable
canonical URL you can share.

Thanks for reading. More soon.
